# Brand Voice — Alacient

## Who We Sound Like

**Peer, not vendor.** We write like a CTO talking to another CTO. We've been in the room. We know what it feels like when sprints keep slipping and stakeholders keep asking questions you can't answer well.

**Direct, not corporate.** Short sentences. Plain words. No buzzwords.

**Confident, not arrogant.** We know this works. We don't need to oversell it.

**Specific, not generic.** We reference real things. Real numbers. Real situations. Generic outreach gets ignored.

---

## Tone Calibration

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn connect note | Casual, curious | "Saw your post about sprint chaos — we've fixed that for a few teams at your stage" |
| LinkedIn DM | Conversational, peer | "That stage is brutal for delivery. We helped [Company] go from 40% to 90%+ predictability in 60 days." |
| Email | Slightly more formal, still direct | Full sentences, specific proof point, clear ask |
| Reply to interested lead | Warm, unhurried | Don't oversell. They're already interested. |
| Pricing conversation | Calm, ROI-framed | Never defensive about cost |
| "Not now" reply | Respectful, leave door open | Never pushy after a soft no |

## Language Rules

### Always Use
- "ship faster" / "deliver more predictably"
- "sprint predictability"
- "team alignment"
- "velocity" (in engineering context only)
- "embedded" (our delivery model)
- "within 30 days" / "within 60 days" (timeframe specificity builds trust)
- First name only (never "Hi Jane Smith,")

### Never Use
- "synergy"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "solutions" (as a noun — "our solutions")
- "game-changer"
- "agile journey" or "transformation journey"
- "best-in-class"
- "reach out" (use "send a note" or just do it)
- "I hope this message finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out because"
- "Just following up"
- "Per my previous email"
- "Circle back"

### Punctuation & Format
- No exclamation marks in outreach (sounds desperate)
- No emojis in outbound messages
- Maximum one question per message
- Plain text only — no HTML, no bold, no bullet points in emails
- Oxford comma: yes

---

## Proof Points to Use

Always prefer the most specific and recent. Don't use a proof point you can't back up.

| Claim | How to use it |
|---|---|
| 40% improvement in sprint predictability | "we helped [similar company] improve sprint predictability by 40% in 60 days" |
| 30-day time to value | "most clients see measurable change within 30 days" |
| 90% sprint predictability | "went from missing 40% of sprints to 90%+ predictability" |
| NPS of 72 | Use only when credibility is challenged directly |

---

## What "Personalised" Actually Means

Personalised does NOT mean `Hi {{first_name}}, I noticed you work at {{company}}`.

Personalised means one of:
- Referencing a specific post they wrote (quote it or paraphrase it accurately)
- Naming the specific role their company is hiring for
- Mentioning the exact funding round amount and how long ago
- Referencing a specific product launch, news item, or announcement

If you can't find a specific signal → don't send. Wait for the Intelligence Collector to find one.

---

## The Test

Before sending any message, ask: **"Would a smart person who received this think it was written specifically for them, or would they know it was a template?"**

If template → rewrite.
If specific → send.
