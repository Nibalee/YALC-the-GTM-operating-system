## LinkedIn

### What's Working (update as data comes in)

| Pattern | Confidence | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Connect note < 200 chars outperforms longer | hypothesis | Shorter = less work to read, more likely to accept |
| Reference LinkedIn post = higher accept rate | hypothesis | Specific > generic always |
| DM1 < 80 words outperforms longer | hypothesis | Engineers hate walls of text |
| Pain-first opening > solution-first | hypothesis | Lead with their problem, not our service |

### Timing

| Day | Notes |
|---|---|
| Monday | Good — people start week checking LinkedIn |
| Tuesday–Wednesday | Best — mid-week, focused |
| Thursday | Good — still responsive |
| Friday | Avoid — lower engagement |
| Weekend | Never |

### Connect Request Accept Rate Benchmarks

- Industry average: ~25–35%
- Good: > 35%
- Excellent: > 50%
- Review copy if: < 20%

## Multi-Channel (LinkedIn + Email)

### Current Hypothesis

Sending both simultaneously (same day) creates recognition without being pushy.
When a prospect sees a LinkedIn connect AND an email in the same week, the second
touch reinforces the first rather than feeling like spam.

**Validate:** compare reply rates for LinkedIn-only vs LinkedIn+Email cohorts.

## Timing Intelligence

### Best time to send LinkedIn connect requests
- Hypothesis: 09:00–10:00 local time (prospect's timezone)
- Reasoning: top of their day, LinkedIn notifications fresh

### Best time to send emails
- Hypothesis: Tuesday–Thursday, 08:00–09:30 recipient local time
- Avoid: Monday morning (crowded inbox), Friday afternoon (distracted)

### DM timing after connect acceptance
- Wait: 48 hours minimum (builds familiarity, avoids instant-pitch feeling)
- Max: 5 days (too long and the connection is cold)
